FAQs

Does Educ8 cover curriculum requirements?

England:
Your staff will cover the computing curriculum requirements in a clear, easy-to-follow way that is not possible with any other resource. All DfE targets have been set in a progressive table across the year groups, all targets are supported by step by step lesson plans, supporting videos, presentations and differentiation tools. Our assessment tool makes progress within the curriculum easy to monitor. See here for more details on Educ8 and Ofsted. See below for how the plan is tailor made for your technology, as well as the skills and confidence of your staff and students. There is also a comprehensive Online Safety plan included (see below).

Ireland:
The Department of Education wants all schools in Ireland to develop an e-learning plan to embed ICT across teaching and learning; as detailed in the Digital Strategy for Schools and the recent ICT infrastructure grant. There is no other resource available that can provide this for you in the way we can; not only do we provide it but we go above and beyond the requirements in an easy-to-follow, practical plan that is designed for teachers by teachers and grows with your school (see below). The plan includes step-by-step lessons plans, with demo videos, presentations, differentiation and assessment tools and opportunities for integration with all other subject areas (see below). Every school's plan is designed for the technology in their school as well as the skills and confidence of their teachers. There is also a comprehensive Online Safety plan included (see below).

International:
The Educ8 curriculum is all encompassing and will help your young learners grow in three key areas:

  • - Digital Literacy (digital storytelling, animating, filmmaking, eBooks and much more!)
  • - Programming (coding)
  • - IT (computer skills, online skills, cloud computing, digital citizenship

Is there an Online Safety Plan program included?

Yes, there is a comprehensive Online Safety Plan curriculum to help your students develop awareness and skills for using technology responsibly. The program progresses every year with age appropriate lessons to ensure that your students leave primary school as informed digital citizens.


Our teachers have found previous planning materials difficult to follow, how have you addressed this?

Every single lesson plan comes with a demo video to give your staff a visual on what is being described in the lesson plan. We have designed the teacher’s dashboard so they can read the plan and play/ pause/ rewind the demo video simultaneously (if they wish!).

All plans also come with presentations for teaching as well as a teacher’s notes option. Teachers can write notes specific to their class/ individual in their class here. During the lessons the students will have access to a student dashboard to help them support their own learning. The student dashboard gives the students access to the presentation that the teacher has just used (so they can scroll back to a specific slide), demo videos to help remind them of the skill just modelled by the teacher and of course the teacher notes.


How is it aimed at the skills and confidence of our staff and students?

We understand that students in upper primary school don’t have the same skills that the students in lower primary will have when they reach upper primary. We also understand that your teachers are at an entry level when it comes to many of the skills involved in teaching with technology. The school plan is based on a six year development strategy that has been designed to ensure all staff and students start at an appropriate difficulty level; every year as their skills and confidence grow, so too does the plan.


How is the plan tailor made for our school?

The first thing the website does when you start building your plan is find out what technology you have available in your school. We use this knowledge to build you a recommended plan based on apps and programs that are a proven success in other schools.

It doesn’t stop there though. You can then choose to edit the plan, review other available options and swap them around depending on the specific wants/ needs of your staff and students. We have created different levels of learning too to ensure that your students are challenged at the appropriate level depending on their skills development to date.


How many lessons are there to choose from?

To remove the stress of searching for the best apps and programs to deliver your curriculum we will build your plan based on apps and programs that are proven to succeed in other schools. However, if you choose to delve a little further and see what other apps and programs are working in other schools you will find over 300 lesson plans to choose from. And that list is growing every day!


Are there any other services available apart from (the above)?

We can register our subscribers for Google Apps (G Suite) or Office 365 if they need this.

We offer a website building service for our subscribers.

We have a secure offsite backup facility for protecting the vital data at your school.


Is there a way of communicating with like minded educators with Educ8.world?

We have a Community Forum for our subscribers giving them access to joining/ starting threads with other educators around the world. Of course this is unlike any other forum because all contributors will be Educ8 subscribers, so all threads will be relevant to your school’s development.


Can you help with training for staff/ parents/ school community?

We have a training resources section on the website that provides materials to deliver your own staff training/ parents evenings. We also have an ever-growing list of local consultants that we are happy to recommend.


How did you choose the apps and programs used in the plans?

The apps and programs have all been tried and tested in classrooms. We have tested a vast amount more than the ones you will see in the plans in order to provide you with a focussed group of top recommended resources.
