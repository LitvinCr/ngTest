Classroom Management

Using technology can be a distracting exercise for students (and adults!). To ensure students are always listening when you are speaking you can get them to hide the screen when you’re speaking; they can do this by locking a tablet, turning it upside down if it has a case with a lip, closing the lid of a tablet/ laptop, turning the monitor off on a desktop computer. It’s a simple but extremely effective strategy to get the most out the lessons.


Countdown Timers

To further help with classroom management we have suggested using timers to keep the students focussed during every task. Feel free to adjust these timers or repeat them if the students need more time. Try and be vigilant though to make sure they stay on task. You can use the countdown timer on your tablet (if it’s plugged into the whiteboard) or you can use free online timers - just Google ‘online countdown timer’. There’s loads to choose from!

Step by Step Instructions - make them your own!

The plans have been written in an easy to follow step by step structure. Don’t be put off by any long instructions, they are only long because they have been broken down into baby steps. Once you have followed the steps once you will understand.

The schemes are divided into 'steps' rather than 'lessons' (not to be confused with the step by step structure of the plans!). This is because each step may be a singular lesson, or it may take two or three lessons, depending on how you choose to deliver it.

The plans have been written in a way that has been proven to succeed in the classroom, but no one knows your students better than you do. Please adjust the plans according to your teaching methods and your students. Get confident with the scheme before teaching the first lesson. This will make the lessons more effective.

Look out for different ways to extend the learning or to integrate it with other areas of learning that is taking part during the same week/ term/ year. Constantly challenge your students and provoke thought from them, make sure they are leading their own learning.

Integrating the Schemes with your Class Topic

Some schemes of work start with two weeks of generic learning tasks that are unrelated to your class topic. This is designed to give the students the necessary time to learn the basic computing skills while simultaneously developing their knowledge of the class topic in other lesson. You can of course change this if the students are making something about a topic where they already have a good understanding of the facts.


Online Safety

There are separate schemes of work for Online Safety for every year group. However, it is always best to follow these basic rules for being safe online if they have not yet been covered in the Online Safety lessons.

Inform students that the internet, just like the real world, does contain nasty, undesired elements and that it’s best to know how to avoid them and what to do if you encounter them. Using effective search terms should help avoid finding undesirable material. Accidents happen though so if they find something they can follow these simple steps:

1) Get rid of it! If it’s a tablet, close it or turn it upside down; if it’s a laptop, close it, a desktop computer, turn off the monitor. NB pressing ‘Back’ can be slow and ineffective

2) Tell a responsible adult

Recording Voice on Tablets

Locate the physical microphone on the tablet; on all iPads up to iPad Air 1 the mic is located on top of the iPad, in the middle, between the headphone jack and the lock button; it’s a tiny hole. It differs on all other tablets. To record good quality voice the students can work in pairs. The director will hold the tablet with the microphone facing the narrator, about 10 - 15cm from their mouth. Ensure students have written a script and practiced saying the lines with expression before recording. Speak in a loud, clear voice. This will produce very good quality audio, even in a noisy classroom. Give the students access to a quiet area if possible, to get even better quality audio.

Setting up your Dashboard

Login to the site using the details sent to you by email.

Go to My Dashboard

Select Class > Click to create a new class

Give the class a name using their intake year (the year they started school), the academic year and your name; in coming years their new teacher will need to retrieve their information, this will make them easy to identify. For example; I​ ntake 2011 - 2017-18 - Ms Saddique

If you are starting with a class that has already done a year of support with Educ8.world then they are already set-up. Simply retrieve them by searching for them using the above criteria to help you search; start with their intake year; then the most recent academic year and select. If you are looking for previous assessment records you can look for a specific year for that class using the academic year and teacher name to help your search.

Enter the email address that the students will be using as a joint username; you could create a class email address or use your own. Create a shared password.

Next you will choose three courses; one for Computer Science, one for Digital Literacy and one for IT. It is intended that most teachers will choose a course that corresponds with their year group. Please feel free to look inside the lessons and choose a more advanced level if your students are already experienced with the course being suggested and you feel they are ready for a harder challenge. This is the school’s entry level plan; every year the levels will increase until there is an Early Years level and then level 1 - 6 corresponding with the rest of the year groups.

Create your assessment document by entering the names of your students.

Choose Save Changes > Choose Complete Plan from the top of the screen.

Congratulations, your class is now setup!

You can now:

  • Easily assess student progress using the assessment tool
  • Give your class the shared username and password to give them access to their student dashboard
  • Leave teacher comments in your teacher dashboard to appear in the student dashboard during lessons

More about the student dashboard:

The student dashboard has been designed so students can support their own learning and free teachers up to circle the room and support as needed (with as little interruption as possible).

The student dashboard gives the students access to the same presentation that you will use for teaching, the demo videos you watched while reading the lesson plan as well as any comments you leave on your teacher dashboard; these can be whole class comments, targets for specific groups, learning needs, additional extension activities etc.
