Teaching with Technology

Teaching with IT can lead to new issues regarding behaviour and classroom management that aren’t present in other lessons. This can lead to some teachers being less inclined to use technology in lessons.

The Good News!

When technology is used properly in lessons, when it is integrated effectively with the curriculum it will reduce behaviour incidents during lessons. This is because when students are fully engaged and have structured, challenging and fun learning objectives they will tend to focus on these. That’s part of the reason we include timers in our lesson plans.

Strategies
  • Make it clear at the very beginning that the technology being used is to achieve learning objectives; it is simply an alternative to a pen and paper, a learning tool, and not a toy.
  • Only give instructions when there is silence and all students are giving full attention
  • Screens are very distracting (even for the best intentioned of people) - it’s best to have these out of sight when you are speaking.
    • Tablets - ensure students are confident with locating the lock screen button in the first lesson. Whenever you speak ask students to lock the screen immediately, then proceed with teaching/ talking; if the tablet has a lip to protect the screen you could turn them upside down; if they have a cover you could close it.
    • Laptops - students could (a) close the lid, or (b) if closing the lid leads to any issues (logging off etc) it can be best to simply turn the laptop around
    • Desktop computers - turn the monitor off (not the whole computer!)
  • Be strict with the timers - a popular rule we use in the classroom is to countdown out loud for the last ten seconds of the timer. Be clear that you expect all screen to be off/ locked. Any student(s) whose screen is visible when the timer gets to zero will lose one minute from the next task. You can escalate this for repeat offenders. Students tend to learn pretty quickly when they see a tablet get taken away though!

That’s it! There are of course plenty more strategies but we like to keep it simple. Have fun :)
